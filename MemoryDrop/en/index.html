Memory Drop gathers and shows you photos from the same month and day in past years. This day last year, two years ago, and further back — travel back through the years and revisit your memories.

Photos take center stage. Amid soft margins and pastel tones, your photos, Live Photos, and videos gently fall, pile up, and scatter. Photos are read on your device and never sent anywhere.

FEATURES

What you can do

No complicated setup. Open the app, and this day from years past is waiting.

🍃

This day, falling in

Photos from the same date in past years gently fall and pile up. On busy days it becomes a "rain of memories." Let it fall again as many times as you like.

🗓️

Travel back through the years

Last year, two years ago, and earlier. Combine the era with filters like place, people, and pets.

🏔️

Favorites

Gently carry a treasured photo upward into Favorites. It's managed within this app, separate from the Photos app's favorites.

📝

Quiet notes

Jot down how you felt that day. Notes stay within this app, and you choose whether to show them when sharing.

💞

Share from a preview

Always preview before sharing. Choose whether to show the date, place, names, notes, and more before you send.

🔒

Stays on your device

No ads, no tracking. Photos are read on your device and never sent to external servers.

HOW IT WORKS

Getting started

1
Try the demo first

At first we don't ask for photo access. You can experience "On This Day" in demo mode.

2
Start with your own photos

Only when you choose "Start with my photos" do we ask for photo access. Limited access is supported too.

3
This day comes falling in

Photos from the same date in past years gently fall and pile up. If there's nothing from last year, it reaches further back or to nearby days.

4
Tap, and savor

Tap to select, tap again to deselect. Double-tap for full view. Press and hold to line photos up oldest-first. Pinch to zoom or rotate with two fingers, and carry a photo upward to favorite it. Leave a note, check the preview, and share.

☁︎

This app isn't about showing "the photos you took today." It's for revisiting photos from the same month and day in past years — "On This Day."
